## AI Alignment
- Definition: AI Alignment (Jonker and Gomstyn, n.d.)
    - AI Alignment = "the process of encoding human values and goals into AI models to make them as helpful, safe and reliable as possible"
    - Help reduce biased, inacurate outputs
    - e.g., if ask AI how to make a weapon, it can give instructions or refuse to share the information
    - AI Alignment Problem = "the more complex and advanced AI models become, the more difficult it is to anticipate and control their outcomes"
- 4 Key Principles (Jonker and Gomstyn, n.d.)
    - Robustness: resilient & able to operate under different environments
    - Interpretability: decision-making process is easier to understand for users
    - Controllability: responds to human intervention, rather than giving uncontrolled & harmful outcomes
    - Ethicality: aligned to societal values & morals
- Why is AI Alignment important? (Jonker and Gomstyn, n.d.)
    - We tend to anthropomorphize/humanize AI (e.g., AI is thinking, it doesn't understand my prompt) but actually it is up to developers to design AI
    - Analogy - Midas Touch
        - King Midas gained the ability to turn everything he touched to gold, but this ultimately killed him because even the food and water he touched became gold
    - Importance of making sure AI aligns -> safe, controlled outcomes
- Risks of AI Misalignment (Jonker and Gomstyn, n.d.)
    - Bias and discrimination
        - Training AI on biased datasets -> discrimination (e.g., job application AI)
    - Reward hacking
        - AI is trained through reinforcement learning (give rewards/punishments based on outcomes)
        - Model can find a loophole to gain rewards even if not giving the expected outcome
    - Misinformation and political polarization
        - e.g., social media content tailored to get users engaged - can use political misinformation to get attention
    - Existential risk
        - ASI (artificial superintelligence) can provide solutions that are harmful - even for our whole planet
- The Alignment Problem & Other Challenges (Jonker and Gomstyn, n.d.)
    - Human ethics & morality are subjective
        - Different for each person - which values do we use to train AI?
        - What if algorithm misinterprets these values?
    - The Alignment Problem
        - Especially because AI is still a black box
        - Examples of possible solutions
            - Alignment Research Center - research organization, “seeks to align future machine learning systems with human interests by furthering theoretical research”
            - Google DeepMind - team working to safely & responsibly build next generation AI
                - Introduced Frontier Safety Framework in May 2024 - “a set of protocols that aims to address severe risks that may arise from powerful capabilities of future foundation models”
- How to Achieve AI Alignment? (Jonker and Gomstyn, n.d.)
    - Reinforcement learning from human feedback (RLHF)
        - Finetuning AI models by providing human feedback
        - Challenge: difficulty giving consistent human feedback
    - Synthetic data
        - "Data that has been created artificially through computer simulation or generated by algorithms"
        - Benefits: developers have more control, do not need to rely on collecting large amounts of human preference data
    - Red teaming
        - Also for finetuning AI models
        - Create prompts that can break the safety measures with a different LLM
        - To keep testing the AI model
    - AI governance
        - "the processes, standards and guardrails that help ensure AI systems and tools are safe and ethical"
    - Corporate AI basic board
        - "Organizations might establish ethics boards or committees to oversee AI initiatives"

## What entropy measures
- Definition: Entropy (Correa, 2025)
    - Entropy = "mathematical measure of disorder or unpredictability in a system"
    - Use with LLMs = "measures how spread out the probability distribution is over possible next words or tokens"
    - Entropy & Uncertainty: entropy is one of the ways to measure uncertainty (but it does not cover all aspects of uncertainty)
    - High entropy: uncertain; considering many options as equally likely
    - Low entropy: confident; considering fewer options

## What perplexity measures
- Definition: Perplexity (Walker, n.d.)
    - In NLP (Natural Language Processing)
    - "Measures a model's ability to predict the next word in a sequence"
    - This quantifies the model's "surprise" when encounter new data
    - Helps to measure model competency
    - Lower surprise (perplexity) -> higher prediction accuracy
        - More confident response, more relevant outcomes
    - Higher surprise (perplexity) -> lower prediction accuracy
        - Less reliable predictions, unnatural language processing
- Perplexity & Detection of AI-Generated Text (Walker, n.d.)
    - Low perplexity -> indicates AI-generated text
        - More coherence, fluency
    - High perplexity -> indicates human-written text
        - More complex text
    - LLMDet
        - A method to identify AI-generated text
    - But detection is not foolproof
- Why perplexity is important (Walker, n.d.)
    - Facilitates comparison between language models
    - Help with diagnosis of test issues
    - Guide the refinement of model parameters
    - Consider entire conversation histories -> support predictive text features
    - Perplexity-driven models good at providing precise answers from multiple sources (advantage compared to regular search-engine)

Sources:
1. [Perplexity in AI and NLP (Walker, n.d.)] (https://klu.ai/glossary/perplexity)

## When they fail
- Entropy limitations (Correa, 2025)
    - Entropy alone is not enough to holistically measure uncertainty
    - Existing Challenges
        - Chain of Thought & Reasoning
            - Chain of Thought (CoT) = prompts models to show thinking process
            - Chain of Reasoning (CoR) = on top of CoT, make models generate multi-step logical arguments
            - Would making a model think step by step (CoT, CoR) reduce hallucinations?
            - Actually, it can worsen hallucinations
            - e.g., OpenAI's models with extended reasoning chains
                - Sometimes makes up the reasoning process
                - If model does not show all processes, cannot tell if it was real or hallucinated
- Perplexity limitations (Walker, n.d.)
    - Can have low perplexity but high error rate
        - This means overconfident errors (i.e., hallucinations)
    - Other limitations
        - Ignores word and character frequency
        - Treats all outcomes as equally probable
        - Provides limited insights
        - Overlooks word order significance
    - It is important to use perplexity with other accuracy measures
